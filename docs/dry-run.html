Dry-Run Mode

Always review before you commit. --dry-run shows you the candidate list with evidence. No surprises. No regrets.

Measure Twice

Every serious engineering tool with destructive potential has a dry-run mode. terraform plan. rsync --dry-run. git stash before a rebase. The pattern exists because irreversible actions deserve a preview.

Removing someone from your life — or adjusting how much of it they have access to — is irreversible in ways that file deletions usually aren't. Dry-run isn't optional caution. It's mandatory respect for the weight of the decision.

What Dry-Run Shows

Running a scan surfaces every tracked subject, sorted by priority, with the evidence behind their score:

asshole-gc --dry-run
$ asshole-gc --dry-run
Scanning environment... done.
 
Candidates flagged: 2
 
● Dave (Engineering)  — 7 days, confidence 94% — FLAG HIGH
  Top signals: DARVO (91%), Condescension (88%), Pattern Persistence (95%)
 
● Karen (Slack)      — 3 days, confidence 71% — FLAG
  Top signals: Chronic Negativity (74%), Escalation (68%)
 
Run with --run to execute. Use --grace-period to warn first.

How to Read the Results

For each flagged subject you'll see:

  • Consecutive bad days — the streak that triggered the flag. This resets on genuine good behavior.
  • Confidence percentage — how certain the system is that the pattern is real vs. situational.
  • Priority level — MONITOR, REVIEW, FLAG, or FLAG HIGH. Only FLAG and above are candidates for action.
  • Top signals — the three highest-scoring dimensions driving their result. This tells you what kind of problem you're dealing with.

The Review Step

Dry-run output isn't a verdict. It's a briefing. Before you act, ask yourself:

  • Does this match what I actually experience? Or does it feel off?
  • Is there context the system doesn't have — something I know but didn't log?
  • Am I the only data source? Should I get a second perspective?
  • Is the top signal actually the right one, or am I pattern-matching on the wrong thing?
  • Have I tried talking to them directly about this?

If everything checks out and the answer to all of those is "yes, this is accurate" — then you have your answer.

False Positives Without the Review

The estimated false positive rate with dry-run review is under 2%. Without it — without pausing to actually read the results and apply human judgment — that rate climbs to roughly 8%. One in twelve. That's not a risk worth skipping the review to avoid.

The most common false positive cause is context the system doesn't have: a subject going through something significant that you know about but didn't flag. The review step is where you apply that knowledge.

What Dry-Run Doesn't Do

Dry-run doesn't contact anyone. It doesn't save to any server. It doesn't create any record outside your own browser. It's a read-only operation — a look at the state of your data and what the algorithm concludes from it. You are the only one who sees it.
